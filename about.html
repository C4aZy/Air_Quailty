About CleanAir Map

Our Mission

CleanAir Map empowers communities to monitor and report air quality in real-time. We believe access to environmental data is a fundamental right.

How It Works

We collect air quality data from official monitoring stations worldwide and combine it with community reports to provide comprehensive pollution mapping.

Understanding AQI

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a standardized indicator of air quality. Higher values indicate greater health concerns:

  • 0-50 (Good): Air quality is satisfactory
  • 51-100 (Moderate): Acceptable for most people
  • 101-150 (Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups): Children, elderly, and people with respiratory conditions should limit outdoor exposure
  • 151-200 (Unhealthy): Everyone may experience health effects
  • 201-300 (Very Unhealthy): Health alert, everyone may experience serious effects
  • 300+ (Hazardous): Emergency conditions, avoid outdoor activities

Data Sources

Real-time data provided by the World Air Quality Index Project and community contributions.
